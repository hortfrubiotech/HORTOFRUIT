»Research Lines

 

Our group has stablished several research lines related with the use of biotechnological methods in plant breeding programs of species with high agronomical importance. The work includes the in vitro micro-propagation, tissue regeneration by both somatogenesis and embriogenesis, and the genetic modification. In this regards, we use a fundamentally empirical approach, like functional genomics of interesting genes, as well as an apply approach with genes that improve the agronomics characteristic of the crop. The main current research lines are described below:

Study of genes involve in fruit texture during post-harvest period, as well as in fungal pathogens tolerance, by means of strawberry genetic transformation.

Somatic embryogenesis and olive transformation with oil quality and pathogen tolerance related genes.
Somatic embryogenesis and avocado transformation with fungal tolerance related genes.
Paulownia sp micropropagation.
