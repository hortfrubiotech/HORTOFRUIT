» Genetic modification of strawberry (Fragaria spp.) plants with genes that extend shelf life and improve their response against fungal pathogens

 

Fungi infection and the fruit quality lost, specially the fruit softening following ripening, are the two main problems in the strawberry production. The reduction in the fruit firmness is related with modifications in the cell wall propierties. With the goal of extending the postharvest fruit shelf life, we are silencing the genes that encode the proteins related with the cell wall modifications. Our results shows a significant increase in fruit firmness in some of the lines. Alternatively, to increase the plant tolerance agains pathogens, we are using genes from the same strawberry plant, but up-regulating their expression, as well as genes from other antagonistic fungi.
